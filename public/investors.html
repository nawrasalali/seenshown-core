📄 SAFE Agreement — Simple Agreement for Future Equity
▼

1. Nature of Investment

This SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is governed by the laws of New South Wales, Australia, and is subject to the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth). This instrument is not a share, debenture, or managed investment scheme. It is a contractual right to receive equity upon a triggering event.

2. Valuation Cap

The post-money valuation cap is AUD $8,000,000. Upon a priced equity round (Series A or equivalent), this SAFE will convert to equity at the lower of (a) the cap price or (b) the Series A price multiplied by 0.80 (the 20% discount).

3. Pro-Rata Rights

Investors holding a SAFE from this round have the right (but not the obligation) to participate in the next priced round up to their pro-rata percentage of the post-money capitalisation.

4. Information Rights

SeenShown (A.C.N. 675 668 710 PTY LTD) will provide quarterly written updates including user metrics, revenue, and material business developments to all SAFE holders.

5. No Guaranteed Returns

This investment carries significant risk. There is no guarantee of returns. You may lose your entire investment. The company makes no representation about future valuation, revenue, or liquidity events.

6. Liquidity Event

Upon acquisition, IPO, or dissolution, SAFE holders will receive the greater of their investment amount or their pro-rata share of proceeds, subject to applicable law.

7. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of New South Wales, Australia. Disputes will be resolved by arbitration in Sydney under ACICA rules.
